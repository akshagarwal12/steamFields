Electric fields as fluid flow

Positive charges behave like fluid sources, negative charges like sinks. Field lines are streamlines. The math underneath is identical.

In 2D, both systems satisfy the same Laplace equation outside their sources. Maxwell himself used this analogy when developing intuition for electromagnetism in the 1860s.
